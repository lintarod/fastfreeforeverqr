The 10:1 Rule Explained

The industry standard for QR code sizing is the 10:1 ratio: divide the scanning distance by 10 to get the minimum QR code size.

QR Size = Scanning Distance √∑ 10
Example: 3 feet away ‚Üí QR code should be at least 3.6 inches

This formula assumes:

  • Standard smartphone camera (most phones since 2018)
  • Decent lighting conditions
  • Medium error correction level
  • Good contrast (dark QR on light background)

Pro tip: When in doubt, go 20-25% larger than the minimum. It costs nothing extra to print bigger, but a QR code that won't scan is worthless.

Tips for Scannable QR Codes

‚úì High Contrast

Dark code on light background works best. Avoid low-contrast color combinations.

‚úì Quiet Zone

Leave white space around the QR code equal to 4x the size of one module (small square).

‚úì Test Before Print

Always scan your QR code with multiple phones before sending to print.

‚úó Don't Invert Colors

Light QR codes on dark backgrounds can cause scanning issues on older phones.

‚úó Don't Over-Decorate

Logos in the center are fine with high error correction, but don't cover more than 30%.

‚úó Don't Stretch

QR codes must be perfectly square. Stretching or skewing breaks them.
